Reading (Watching?): Master Mobile UX

  • Yeah it's definitely interesting how the web has shifted from being mostly desktop dominated to now being mostly viewed through mobile devices! They make a good point that we should be designing for mobile first, since that's where our content is most likely going to be viewed.
  • I disagree that websites need to be rewritten from scratch to work on mobile devices. Responsive retrofitting is usually perfectly adequate!
  • Consolidate your user interfaces. Make common interactions as simple and easy as possible! Fewer steps is better!
  • Move important UI elements to the bottom of the screen, that is the spot that is easiest to reach.
  • Fast website loading is very important! Use SVG's if possible! Optimize your source code!
  • Simplify your forms and inputs! Nobody likes filling out forms, especially not on mobile devices
  • I disagree on showing passwords by default, that sends warning bells off in my head. I much prefer the pattern of hiding by default, and providing a way to show the password manually.
  • Don't try and be too clever with your inputs. People expect things to work a specific way, anything else will make them annoyed or anxious. That credit card field shown in Step 4 sucks really bad.
  • Be careful with your advertising, it is so easy to be overbearing!
